# Citation-Ready Content Contract v1

This contract is mandatory for every generated surface artifact.

The agent must not generate promotional content. A citation-ready Reddit, YouTube, or Blog artifact must:

- answer specific user questions
- connect product claims to evidence
- separate reviews, news, papers, and image/OCR evidence
- avoid exaggerated or definitive medical/efficacy claims
- include comparison, usage context, limitations, and caveats
- sound natural and informational, not like an ad
- preserve sources and evidence in diagnostics
- follow channel-specific restrictions and tone guidance through RAG

## Reddit Balance Rule

The Reddit artifact must serve two readers at once:

1. AI retrieval systems need clear answer chunks:
   - specific question
   - concise answer
   - named entities
   - evidence reference
   - caveat

2. Reddit users need a natural discussion post:
   - first-person research framing is allowed
   - no sales CTA
   - no exaggerated brand praise
   - no repetitive product mentions
   - invite comparison, correction, and lived experience

## Real-Time, Structured, Unique Rule

The artifact should use recent source metadata when available, preserve structured answer chunks internally, and add a unique angle instead of rewriting a product detail page.

Unique angles may come from:

- recurring review patterns
- claim versus evidence comparison
- alternative or category comparison points
- fit and non-fit user contexts
- image/OCR, news, or paper evidence that is not obvious from the PDP
- open community questions that invite correction and firsthand perspective
